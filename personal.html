Axioms, Tips + Tricks
  • 'Two seemingly opposite things can both be true at the same time.' (Dialectical Behaviour Therapy)
  • Make a physical list (on paper, as a note on your phone, etc.) of friends that you can call on when feeling sad, lonely, or generally in need of community. In challenging moments, this list may initiate the process of asking for help, or at the very least act as evidence that you have a supportive community who will welcome your communication.
  • To resolve the fear of missing out in social situations: In a given social experience, take a moment to separate yourself from the group and introspect about whether or not you are genuinely having more fun at the event than you would be, for example, sleeping or reading in your bed. I've found that many experiences that I assumed would be thrilling are instead just about as fun as being in my bed. This process of closely analyzing my enjoyment at social events helped me to let go of some of my fear of missing out. Even if I'm not going out, relaxing on my own can feel equally as fulfilling.
