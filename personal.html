Axioms, Tips + Tricks

Note: I learned about many of the items below from my dear friends and mentors. I want to share these bits of wisdom in case others may benefit from them, but I do not take credit for their existence.
  • 'Emotional intelligence is a scientific advantage.'
  • I keep with me at all times a pouch of self-care resources from Small Supply. These resources include a series of zines with a) strategies for navigating moments of anxiety, b) affirmations and encouragement, and c) reminders of self-compassion. Even when I'm not actively using them, it is calming to have some on-hand resources for managing stressful situations.
  • 'Two seemingly opposite things can both be true at the same time.'
  • To remember your community: Make a physical list (on paper, as a note on your phone, etc.) of friends that you can call on when feeling sad, lonely, or generally in need of community. In challenging moments, this list may initiate the process of asking for help, or at the very least act as evidence that you have a supportive community who will welcome your communication.
  • To resolve the fear of missing out: In a given social experience, take a moment to separate yourself from the group and introspect about whether or not you are genuinely having more fun at the event than you would be, for example, sleeping or reading in your bed. I've found that many experiences that I assumed would be thrilling are instead just about as fun as being in my bed. This process of closely analyzing my enjoyment at social events helped me to let go of some of my fear of missing out. Even if I'm not going out, relaxing on my own can feel equally fulfilling.
